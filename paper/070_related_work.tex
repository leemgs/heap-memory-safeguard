\section{Related Mechanisms}
\label{sec_related}
\paragraph{Runtime and allocation telemetry.}
ART exposes collector diagnostics and Perfetto's \texttt{heap\-profd} samples native
allocations and deallocations with call stacks~\cite{AOSP_ART_GC,Perfetto_Heapprofd}.
Sampling has eligibility, buffer, and accuracy constraints, so a deployment must
measure its overhead and dropped data. HMS proposes joining such telemetry with
resident state; it does not replace ART accounting or claim complete native-object
semantics.

\paragraph{Android pressure handling.}
LMKD has used PSI-based pressure detection since Android 10 and incorporates
thrashing and process importance in kill decisions~\cite{AOSP_LMKD}. Android also
provides cached-process freezing, compaction, and newer per-process zRAM
writeback/prefetch paths~\cite{AOSP_CachedApps,AOSP_MMD}. These are stronger and
more direct baselines than unrelated storage or disaggregated-memory work. HMS is
positioned as a proposed pre-kill, per-application budgeting policy that must be
compared against tuned versions of these mechanisms.

\paragraph{Linux proactive reclaim.}
Cgroup v2 exposes \texttt{memory.reclaim}, including reclaim amount and optional
swappiness controls~\cite{Linux_CgroupV2}. \texttt{DAMON\_RECLAIM} identifies cold regions
and supports proactive reclaim under configurable watermarks and quotas
~\cite{Linux_DAMONReclaim}. MGLRU provides working-set estimation, thrashing
protection, and proactive eviction controls~\cite{Linux_MGLRU}. HMS adds no new
page-selection algorithm in this revision; its research question is whether
runtime-informed timing and budgets improve these existing actuators.

\paragraph{Memory tagging.}
Android documents MTE as a mechanism for detecting use-after-free and bounds
violations, with synchronous, asynchronous, and asymmetric modes
~\cite{AOSP_MTE}. MTE faults are therefore excluded from the HMS pressure score.

\paragraph{Positioning.}
The remaining novelty claim is deliberately narrow: an online state model and
bounded, fair trigger that coordinates existing telemetry and reclaim interfaces.
Demonstrating superiority requires the baseline and device study specified in
Section~\ref{sec_eval}. The simulation evidence alone cannot establish that claim.
